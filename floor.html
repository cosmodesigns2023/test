How Floor Tiles Are Laid: A Comprehensive Guide by Advanced Decor ?


Are you considering a home renovation project or sprucing up your commercial space? One crucial element that can completely transform the look and feel of your interiors is the flooring. Among the various flooring options available, tiles are a popular choice due to their durability, versatility, and aesthetic appeal. In this comprehensive guide by Advanced Decor, we'll walk you through the process of how floor tiles are laid, ensuring you have all the information you need for your upcoming project.


Step 1: Surface Preparation
Before the tile installation process begins, it's essential to prepare the surface properly. Here's how it's done:
a. Subfloor Inspection: The existing subfloor, whether concrete or wooden, must be thoroughly inspected for cracks, unevenness, or structural issues. Any problems should be addressed and repaired.
b. Clean and Remove Debris: The subfloor is cleaned to remove any dirt, dust, or debris. A clean surface ensures better adhesion and prevents irregularities in the finished surface.
c. Leveling: If the subfloor has uneven spots or minor imperfections, they are leveled using a self-leveling compound. This step ensures a flat and even surface for tile installation.
Step 2: Planning and Layout
Proper planning and layout are essential for achieving a visually appealing and symmetrical tile installation:
a. Determine the Layout: Decide on the tile layout pattern, whether it's a classic grid, diagonal, herringbone, or custom design. Ensure it complements the room's dimensions and aesthetics.
b. Measure and Mark: Precisely measure the room's dimensions and mark the layout lines using chalk or pencil. These lines act as guidelines for tile placement.
c. Dry Layout: Before adhering tiles with mortar, perform a dry layout by placing tiles along the layout lines. This helps visualize the final look and make any necessary adjustments.
Step 3: Mixing Mortar or Adhesive The type of mortar or adhesive used depends on the tile material, location, and subfloor. Follow these steps:
a. Select the Appropriate Mortar: Choose mortar or adhesive that is compatible with your tile type (e.g., thin-set mortar for ceramic tiles or epoxy mortar for heavy-duty applications).
b. Mix According to Instructions: Follow the manufacturer's instructions to prepare the mortar or adhesive. Use the correct water-to-mix ratio and mix it to a uniform consistency.

Step 4: Tile Installation
Now comes the exciting part - laying the tiles:
a. Apply Mortar: Using a notched trowel, spread a layer of mortar evenly on a small section of the subfloor within the layout lines.
b. Set the Tiles: Carefully place each tile onto the mortar, pressing it firmly to ensure good adhesion. Use spacers to maintain consistent gaps between tiles.
c. Cut Tiles as Needed: For edges and corners, you may need to cut tiles to fit. A tile cutter or wet saw is typically used for this purpose.
d. Continue in Sections: Work in small sections, repeating the mortar application and tile installation process until the entire floor is covered.
Step 5: Grouting
After the tiles are set and the mortar has dried, it's time to fill the gaps between the tiles with grout:
a. Choose Grout: Select a grout color that complements your tile and desired aesthetic. Mix the grout according to the manufacturer's instructions.
b. Apply Grout: Use a rubber float to press the grout into the gaps between tiles at a 45-degree angle. Remove excess grout from the tile surface using a damp sponge.
c. Cure and Seal: Allow the grout to cure for the recommended time, typically 24-48 hours. Afterward, apply a grout sealer to protect it from staining and moisture.
Step 6: Finishing Touches
Complete the installation with these final touches:
a. Clean the Surface:Thoroughly clean the tiles to remove any remaining grout haze or adhesive residue.
b. Apply Sealant (Optional): Depending on the tile material, you may choose to apply a tile and grout sealer to enhance durability and stain resistance.
c. Install Baseboards: Install baseboards or trim to cover the edges of the tiles and create a polished finish.


Congratulations! You've successfully learned how floor tiles are laid. Whether you're tackling a DIY project or hiring professionals like Advanced Decor, proper tile installation can greatly enhance the beauty and functionality of your space.
